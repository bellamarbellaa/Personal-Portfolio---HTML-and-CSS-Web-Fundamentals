Sales by Salesperson Analysis Project in Google Workspace

In this project, I took a deep dive into sales data of fictional salespeople across different American states, turning numbers into actionable insights that can support data-driven decision making. Here is the summary of my works in this project:

  • State-by-State Breakdown: I created a summary table to show things like total sales, average sales, and the highest and lowest sales per state. It is a quick way to see which states are leading the pack.

  • Custom Calculations: Using multi-condition formulas in Google Sheets, I calculated specific KPIs, like average sales per client and per salesperson, in order to address specific business questions.

  • Visual Insights: To make it all easier to understand, I made a bar chart comparing sales across states, which helps highlight top performers at a glance.

Movies Data Analysis in Google Workspace

This project aims to analyze factors impacting box office success of popular movies. Using a dataset of films that includes each movie's title, release date, primary and secondary genres, director(s), cast members, production budget, and box office revenue, I conducted a thorough analysis to reveal patterns in profitability and spending. Here is the summary of my works in this project:

  • Interactive Dashboard: I built an intuitive dashboard displaying key metrics, such as yearly and monthly revenue trends, genre performance, and top directors, allowing for a quick, visual understanding of what drives movie profitability.

  • Data Sorting, Cleaning, and Formatting: I cleaned, structured, and sorted the dataset, covering multiple genres and cast members per movie, along with revenue and budget figures, ensuring that all data was organized and ready for effective analysis.

  • Pivot Tables Analysis: I used pivot tables to break down revenue by dimensions like release year, director, and genre. This analysis helped identify high-performing directors and genres, as well as trends in budget allocation and revenue generation.

Penguin Dataset Analysis in R Studio

This project focused on cleaning, organizing, and visualizing the Penguins Dataset using R, showcasing a range of data manipulation and visualization techniques. Working with R tidyverse packages, I transformed the raw data into a structured and insightful analysis of penguin species, including various visualizations that provide a clear view of trends and patterns within the dataset. Here I summarize my works in this project:

  • Data Cleaning and Preparation: I meticulously cleaned the dataset, handling missing values, correcting data types, and standardizing formats to ensure consistency. This step was critical for improving data quality and ensuring accurate downstream analysis.

  • Organizing and Transforming Data for Analysis: I organized and reshaped the data to facilitate analysis, using tools in R to create a well-structured dataset that supports various visualizations. This included transforming variables, creating derived metrics, and segmenting data by species, islands, and other attributes.

  • Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA): I conducted EDA to uncover initial insights, such as distributions and relationships between variables like flipper length, body mass, and species type. I utilized summary statistics and preliminary plots to guide further analysis.

  • Data Visualization: I created a series of visualizations to effectively communicate insights. This included scatter plots, box plots, and density plots, each tailored to highlight specific relationships within the data. The visualizations provide a comprehensive view of species characteristics and comparisons across different penguin groups.

  • Beyond The Basic - Data Storytelling: Beyond basic plots, I customized visual elements, including color schemes, labels, and themes, to make the data story more accessible and visually appealing. Each visualization is crafted to emphasize key findings and trends in the data, making it easy for viewers to understand the analysis.

  • Insights and Conclusion: Derived insights on species differences, environmental factors, and potential drivers behind physical characteristics. This project demonstrates my ability in utilziing R for data manipulation, cleaning, and visualization in producing meaningful analyses and deriving key insights from large volume of data.

World Happiness Score 2015-2020 Comparison Dashboard in Tableu

This project is a comprehensive analysis of global happiness scores over a five-year period using Tableau. I created an interactive dashboard that visualizes trends, regional comparisons, and correlations between happiness scores and key indicators like economic factors, social support, and life expectancy. The dashboard allows users to explore data interactively, providing a clear and accessible view of global happiness trends. Here is the summary of my works in this project:

  • Data Import and Preparation: I imported and cleaned data within Tableau, ensuring consistency across multiple years to allow for accurate comparisons and trend analysis.

  • Dynamic Dashboard Design: I developed an interactive dashboard that features filters, hover-over tooltips, and drill-down capabilities, enabling users to explore happiness scores by region, year, and various socio-economic factors.

  • Insight Visualization: Key Indicators of World Happiness I created charts and graphs to illustrate the impact of GDP, social support, life expectancy, and other factors on happiness scores, showing clear correlations and trends.

  • Year-after-year Trend Analysis I designed visuals to display happiness score changes over time, highlighting regions with significant changes and enabling easy comparison between countries and years.

  • User-centric Data Storytelling Structured the dashboard to guide users through the data, emphasizing the most impactful insights and trends. Each element is organized to create a cohesive narrative of how happiness levels vary globally and over time. Finally, I included filters and custom tooltips that allow users to explore specific countries or regions, providing a personalized data exploration experience.
